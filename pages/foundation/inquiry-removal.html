What We Do

Inquiries Matter More Than Most People Realize

When lenders see 8–12 hard inquiries on your report, they see risk — even if your score is decent. Multiple recent inquiries suggest you've been aggressively seeking credit, which many lenders interpret as a sign of financial stress.

The good news: not all inquiries are legitimate, and not all legitimate ones are permanent. We review every inquiry on your report, identify which can be challenged, and dispute them with the bureaus on your behalf.

  • Full inquiry audit across all 3 credit bureaus
  • Identification of unauthorized or fraudulent inquiries
  • Dispute of inquiries without proper permissible purpose
  • Challenge of duplicate or excessive inquiry records
  • Coordination with inquiry removal and credit stacking strategy
  • Documentation support for FCRA dispute filings

FAQ

Inquiry Removal Questions

Can all hard inquiries be removed?

+
Not all inquiries can be removed. Authorized inquiries from legitimate credit applications typically stay for 2 years. However, unauthorized inquiries, those pulled without proper permissible purpose, or inquiries resulting from identity theft can be legally disputed and removed. We'll tell you upfront which ones we can challenge.

How long does inquiry removal take?

+
Credit bureaus have 30–45 days to respond to disputes. Most inquiry removals are resolved within 30–60 days of filing. We follow up proactively if bureaus don't respond within the required window.

How much will my score improve?

+
Each hard inquiry typically costs 2–10 points. Removing 6–10 inquiries can yield a 20–50 point improvement. The exact impact depends on your overall profile — inquiries hurt more when your score is lower and your file is thinner.

Is this service often combined with credit repair?

+
Yes — most clients benefit from both. Credit repair addresses negative items like collections and late payments, while inquiry removal clears the inquiry noise. We often run both simultaneously to maximize your score improvement timeline before transitioning to funding.
