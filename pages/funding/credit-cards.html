Who This Is For

Credit Cards Work Best When...

🚀

You're a Startup or New Business

Traditional lenders want 2 years of business history. Business credit cards often rely primarily on personal credit — making them one of the best options for newer businesses with strong personal credit.

💡

You Need Flexible, Revolving Capital

Unlike a term loan, a credit card is revolving — you pay down and reuse. For ongoing expenses like marketing, inventory, or payroll, that flexibility is invaluable at 0% interest.

🎯

You Want Interest-Free Growth Capital

If you can deploy the capital and generate returns within 12–18 months, borrowing at 0% is essentially free money. We help you plan the strategy around the promotional window.

FAQ

Credit Card Questions

What happens when the 0% period ends?

+
After the promotional period, the standard APR kicks in — typically 18–28% depending on the card and your credit. We advise you on how to plan around this: either pay off the balance before the period ends, transfer the balance, or refinance into a lower-rate product before the deadline.

What credit score do I need?

+
Most 0% business credit card programs require a personal credit score of 670+. Some entry-level options exist for scores as low as 620. The higher your score, the higher the limits you're likely to be approved for. If your score is below 670, we typically recommend our Foundation program first.

Will applying hurt my credit score?

+
Each credit card application generates a hard inquiry, which can temporarily reduce your score by 2–10 points. We minimize this by batching applications strategically — and in many cases, we clear existing inquiries before we apply so your profile starts clean.

Can I get multiple cards at once?

+
Yes — this is the foundation of our credit stacking strategy. By applying to multiple issuers within the same 30-day window, we limit the number of inquiry reporting cycles while maximizing total approved credit. See our Credit Stacking page for more details on this strategy.
