Who This Is For

A Term Loan Makes Sense When...

🔨

You Have a Specific, Defined Investment

Renovating a location, launching a new product line, acquiring equipment — a term loan with fixed payments lets you match the repayment to the expected return timeline.

📋

You Want Predictable Monthly Payments

Unlike a BLOC or credit card, a term loan has a fixed payment each month. If you prefer knowing exactly what you owe and when you'll be debt-free, a term loan provides that clarity.

🏦

You Need Larger Amounts Than Cards Offer

Need $150K–$500K? Credit cards typically max out below that. Term loans can reach significantly higher amounts for qualified borrowers with strong revenue and credit.

FAQ

Term Loan Questions

What documents do I need for a term loan?

+
Typical documentation includes 3–6 months of business bank statements, the most recent 1–2 years of business tax returns, a government-issued ID, and basic business information (EIN, business license). Some lenders also request accounts receivable aging reports or profit & loss statements. We advise you on exactly what each lender needs before you apply.

Can I pay off a term loan early?

+
Most of the lenders in our network don't charge prepayment penalties, meaning you can pay off the loan early to save on interest. However, some lenders do charge a fee or require a minimum interest payment period. We flag this clearly during the offer review phase so you know what you're agreeing to.

How is a term loan different from an SBA loan?

+
SBA loans are a type of term loan — but they're partially guaranteed by the federal government, which allows lenders to offer larger amounts at lower rates and longer repayment terms. However, SBA loans have more documentation requirements and take longer to process. Conventional term loans are faster and less paperwork-intensive but typically carry higher rates and shorter terms.

What's a realistic interest rate I should expect?

+
Rates vary significantly based on your credit score, revenue, time in business, and loan amount. Strong borrowers (720+ credit, solid revenue, 2+ years in business) can access rates of 8–14% APR. Fair credit or shorter history typically lands in the 18–28% range. We always advise you to compare the total cost of the loan — not just the rate — before accepting any offer.
