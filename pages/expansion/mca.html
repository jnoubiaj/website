An Important Note on MCAs

Merchant Cash Advances are fast and accessible — but they carry high effective rates (often 1.1–1.5 factor rates). We recommend MCAs only when the use case genuinely justifies the cost, or when other options aren't available. We'll always show you the full cost comparison before recommending one.

What We Do

Fast Capital When You Need It — With Full Cost Transparency

A Merchant Cash Advance is not a loan — it's a purchase of your future receivables. The MCA provider gives you capital now in exchange for a percentage of your daily credit card or bank deposits until the advance plus fees are repaid.

We work with MCA providers who offer transparent terms and competitive factor rates. More importantly, we help you understand whether an MCA is actually the right tool for your situation — or whether a better, lower-cost option exists.

  • $10,000–$1,000,000+ in funding
  • Funded in 24–48 hours for qualified applicants
  • Repayment as a fixed percentage of daily revenue
  • No fixed monthly payment — slows when revenue slows
  • No minimum credit score required — some lenders have no credit floor
  • Transparent factor rate disclosure before you sign

When It Makes Sense

MCAs Have a Role — In the Right Situation

🚨

Time-Sensitive Opportunity or Need

A major contract just came in and you need capital within 48 hours to fulfill it. The revenue from the contract far exceeds the cost of the MCA. In these cases, speed justifies the premium.

📉

Credit Has Ruled Out Other Options

If your credit profile doesn't qualify for traditional products and you have strong revenue, an MCA may be your only immediate access to capital while you work on your credit profile.

🔄

Short Bridge While Better Funding Closes

You're in the middle of an SBA application (60 days out) but need capital now. An MCA can bridge the gap — as long as the numbers work and the timeline is clear.

FAQ

MCA Questions

Is a Merchant Cash Advance a loan?

+
Technically, no. An MCA is a purchase of your future receivables — not a loan. This means it's not regulated the same way as loans, and MCA providers don't have to disclose APRs the same way lenders do. This is why we always calculate and show you the effective APR equivalent before you sign anything.

What revenue do I need to qualify?

+
Most MCA providers want to see at least $10,000–$15,000 in average monthly deposits over the last 3–6 months. Some providers work with businesses doing as little as $5,000/month. The advance amount is typically 50–150% of your average monthly revenue.

What is a factor rate and how do I calculate the real cost?

+
A factor rate is multiplied by the advance amount to determine your total repayment. If you receive $100,000 at a 1.35 factor rate, you repay $135,000 — regardless of how quickly you repay it. Unlike interest, a factor rate doesn't decrease if you pay early. The effective APR depends on how fast you repay: faster repayment = higher effective APR. We walk you through this math before you commit.

Can I get an MCA while working on better credit?

+
Yes — and this is one of the more strategic uses of an MCA. If you need capital now but are in the middle of our Foundation program, an MCA can bridge the gap. We structure it intentionally so that by the time the MCA is repaid, your credit is ready for a much better product. We do not recommend stacking MCAs or renewing repeatedly.
