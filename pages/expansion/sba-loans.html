SBA loans are notoriously complex — but for businesses that qualify, they offer unmatched terms: rates as low as prime + 2.75%, repayment periods up to 25 years, and loan amounts up to $5 million. The problem is most business owners don't know how to position themselves correctly or navigate the paperwork.

We act as your guide through the entire SBA process — from credit preparation and document assembly through lender matching, application submission, and closing.

FAQ

SBA Loan Questions

What is the difference between SBA 7(a) and SBA 504?

+
SBA 7(a) is the most common program — it can be used for working capital, equipment, real estate, business acquisition, and more. It goes up to $5M. SBA 504 is specifically for major fixed assets like commercial real estate or large equipment — it's structured as two loans (one from a lender, one from a Certified Development Company) and often offers even lower rates for eligible projects.

What credit score do I need for an SBA loan?

+
Most SBA lenders want to see a personal credit score of at least 650. However, lenders also evaluate business credit, revenue, profitability, debt service coverage, and collateral. A strong 680 with excellent financials may outperform a 720 with marginal cash flow. We assess your complete picture — not just the score.

How long does the SBA process take?

+
Standard SBA loans typically take 30–90 days from application to funding. SBA Express loans (up to $500K) can be approved in as little as 36 hours, though funding still takes 1–2 weeks. The timeline depends heavily on how prepared your documentation is — which is exactly why we front-load that work before you apply.

What if I've been turned down by a bank for an SBA loan?

+
A rejection from one SBA lender doesn't mean you don't qualify — it means that lender wasn't the right fit or your application needed work. We've helped many clients get SBA loans after being turned down elsewhere, by identifying the right lender for their profile and fixing the issues that caused the original rejection.
